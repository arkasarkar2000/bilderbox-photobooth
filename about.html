What is Bilderbox?

Bilderbox (from German Bilder — pictures, and box — well, box) is a free, open browser-based photobooth inspired by the analog film photography aesthetic and the photo booth trend sweeping social media.

There's something magical about a physical photo strip — the format, the imperfection, the instant memory. Bilderbox brings that feeling to the web without needing an app, an account, or an internet connection after the first load.

What it does

Real-time capture
Live viewfinder with single shot and burst mode (4 photos).
Self-timer
3, 5, or 10 second countdown so you can get in the frame.
11 filters
B&W, Sepia, Vivid, Fade, Noir, Warm, Disposable, Kodak, Fujifilm, Y2K.
4 layouts
Classic strip, 2×2 grid, film reel, and scattered polaroid.
Download & share
Save as strip, reel, or grid. QR code sharing included.
Installs as a PWA
Add to home screen on any device. Works offline once installed.

Privacy by design

Bilderbox was designed from the ground up to not collect any data. This isn't a legal technicality — there is genuinely no infrastructure to send data to. Everything happens in your browser, in memory, and disappears when you close the tab.
